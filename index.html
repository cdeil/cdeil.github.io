Christoph Deil

I am a gamma-ray astronomy researcher working at the Max-Planck-Institute for Nuclear Physics in Heidelberg, Germany. My main scientific interest is to discover and try to understand cosmic accelerators in our Galaxy, the Milky way, via the H.E.S.S. Galactic plane survey and complementary observations with other telescopes.

In the past few years, I have started a set of complementary projects with the goal of moving gamma-ray astronomy towards open data and tools: gamma-astro-data-formats, gamma-cat, Gammapy and gamma-sky.net.

I have a diploma in physics and PhD in astronomy from Heidelberg university, a strong background in data science and software development, and I'm looking for an interesting and challenging job near Heidelberg. You can find me on GitHub, or LinkedIn.
